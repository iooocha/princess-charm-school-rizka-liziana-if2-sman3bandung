School Activity

Dance Practice

Dance practice at Princess Charm School is an important activity where students learn various styles of formal dancing, especially ballroom. These lessons prepare them for royal events, such as the Royal Ball, where grace and poise are essential. Students practice coordination, elegance, and rhythm, ensuring they can move confidently in social and ceremonial settings.

Art and Crafting

Art and Craft at Princess Charm School encourages students to explore their creativity through various artistic projects. These activities include painting, sculpting, and designing royal-themed crafts such as crowns or gowns. Students use their imagination to create beautiful works while learning patience and precision.

Debate Club

Debate Club at Princess Charm School is a platform for students to sharpen their public speaking and critical thinking skills. Participants engage in discussions on various topics, learning how to present arguments persuasively, listen to opposing viewpoints, and engage in respectful debate. The club helps students develop confidence, leadership, and diplomacy—essential traits for future princesses.

Equastrian Training

Equestrian Training at Princess Charm School involves teaching students how to ride and care for horses, a vital skill for future princesses. In these sessions, students learn proper riding techniques, horse handling, and the etiquette of royal equestrian events. The activity promotes discipline, balance, and respect for animals while helping students gain confidence in their abilities.

Royal Charity Event

The Royal Charity Event at Princess Charm School is an important extracurricular activity where students engage in philanthropic efforts and community service. These events help students understand the importance of giving back to their communities, promoting compassion and empathy—key qualities for future leaders.

Theater and Performance Club

Theater and Performance at Princess Charm School allows students to express themselves creatively while building confidence and public speaking skills. Through plays and performances, students learn to embody different characters, practice stage presence, and improve communication.
